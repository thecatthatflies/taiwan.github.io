Taiwan’s Historical Timeline

Explore the key moments that shaped Taiwan, from ancient settlements to modern democracy. Click through the milestones to learn more.

Ancient Indigenous Settlement

Taiwan’s history begins with Austronesian-speaking peoples who settled the island over 6,000 years ago. Their descendants remain as Taiwan’s Indigenous tribes today.

Dutch and Spanish Colonization (1624–1662)

The Dutch and Spanish both established colonial settlements in Taiwan. These early European presences marked the beginning of Taiwan’s global interactions.

Koxinga Defeats the Dutch (1662)

Ming loyalist Koxinga expelled the Dutch and established the Kingdom of Tungning, a short-lived state that made Taiwan a base for Ming resistance.

Qing Dynasty Rule (1683–1895)

Taiwan became a part of China’s Qing Empire. Settlers from mainland China brought new culture and agricultural practices to the island.

Japanese Rule (1895–1945)

After the First Sino-Japanese War, Taiwan was ceded to Japan. Infrastructure and public health systems improved, but Japanese rule was strict.

Martial Law Begins (1949)

After retreating from mainland China, the Kuomintang government imposed martial law on Taiwan, restricting freedoms for decades.

228 Incident (1947)

A brutal crackdown on protestors led to the deaths of thousands. The event marked the beginning of Taiwan’s White Terror era.

Martial Law Lifted (1987)

Martial law officially ended, allowing the growth of civil liberties, democratic reforms, and the emergence of new political voices.

First Democratic Election (1996)

Taiwan held its first direct presidential election, marking a major milestone in its transition to democracy and global recognition.

Modern Taiwan

Today, Taiwan is a vibrant democracy known for its technology, diverse culture, and complex international status.

Taiwan’s Story

Taiwan’s path from Indigenous roots to colonial rule, and finally to democracy, reflects its resilience and evolution. Each chapter adds depth to the identity of the island and its people.
